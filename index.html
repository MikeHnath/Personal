About
Most people in marketing came up through internships and agency ladders. I came up through a van.
At 17, I started my first band and never looked back. By my mid-20s I was managing international tours across 22 countries, negotiating multiple distribution deals, and figuring out how to sell over $150K in merch out of a trailer. Nobody gave me a playbook. I just had to figure it out — logistics, budgets, branding, audience growth, all of it — in real time.
That instinct — figure it out, own the outcome, ship it — carried me through Kraft Heinz, Pfizer, Disney, and a decade of building brands for founders through my consultancy, THIS. The details are in the work.
Outside of work, I'm usually at the gym, at the beach, or racking up a game of pool. I love creating media of all kinds — music, video, design, anything I can make with my hands or a screen — and I'm at my best when I'm around people. Connecticut's shoreline is home base, and I try not to take that for granted.

Experience
I didn't stumble into different industries — I chose them. Each W2 role was a deliberate skill build: luxury retail production, licensed CPG at scale, enterprise digital shelf, B2B SaaS demand gen, regulated pharma media. Through all of it, THIS ran in the background as a side consultancy. It never stopped. In 2024, it became the full-time primary.
The foundation — music industry, live production, early business
Before marketing was my career, music was my business. I started a band in college, booked 350+ shows across North America, grew our following to 20K organically, and landed two distribution deals and a Mesa/Boogie endorsement — all self-managed, no label, no safety net. When the chance came to tour-manage Within the Ruins across 22 countries through eOne Music, I said yes and figured out international logistics, visa paperwork, and border crossings on the fly.

That period taught me something no degree could: when you're the only one responsible for the outcome, you learn fast or you fail publicly.

01 · Context
A regulated cannabis brand entering multiple state markets with no visual identity, no investor materials, and no production infrastructure.
Scandalous came in as a cannabis brand with strong product conviction and zero brand scaffolding. The challenge was building a visual system and market presence that could survive multi-state regulatory review, attract wholesale and licensing partners, and raise institutional capital — simultaneously.

Regulated consumer packaging is unforgiving: every state has different labeling requirements, warning language, sizing constraints, and compliance checkpoints. The brand had to flex across those requirements without losing visual coherence.
02 · Action
Built the full brand system, packaging architecture, and investor-facing materials from scratch.
Developed brand identity, typography, color system, and voice. Designed 11–13 SKUs of packaging across WA, OR, CA, IL, and NY — each state-compliant while maintaining visual brand coherence. Built investor decks, pitch materials, and a website that raised $500K+.

Developed the licensing and distribution pitch framework used to initiate partnerships across five states, including the active NY/Brooklyn expansion.
03 · Result
$500K+ raised. Multi-state operations established. Anchor retainer relationship sustained for nine years.
The brand system I built directly supported $500K+ in investor funding. 11–13 SKUs deployed across five regulated state markets. A consistent visual identity maintained across all compliance requirements. Six additional SKUs currently in the active pipeline.
04 · Ongoing
Nine-year client relationship, active expansion into New York.
Scandalous remains the anchor retainer for THIS (~$15K/yr). NY/Brooklyn licensing partnership is an active warm intro. Six SKUs in development pipeline. The relationship has sustained through every phase of THIS — from side consultancy through full-time primary business.

01 · Context
Top-tier pharma clients requiring regulated paid media management across programmatic and social — with legal and MLR sign-off on every asset.
POCN is a healthcare marketing platform serving pharmaceutical brands. Every campaign asset required legal/MLR review before deployment. At peak, I was managing 25 concurrent campaigns simultaneously across Meta, LinkedIn, and Lasso DSP for Eli Lilly, Pfizer, and Johnson & Johnson. Simultaneously, engineering was building a proprietary internal DSP to replace Lasso/MediaOS.
02 · Action
End-to-end campaign management, DSP migration leadership, and performance storytelling for pharma clients.
Handled full campaign lifecycle in Lasso DSP: audience segmentation, campaign setup, pacing, optimization, and performance reporting. Served as power user and migration lead on the internal DSP rollout — tested all 25 active campaigns, migrated from Lasso once stable, and worked directly with the white-label provider to surface UX gaps and validate fixes.

Translated campaign data into messaging frameworks, positioning recommendations, and client-facing performance narratives that protected and grew accounts.
03 · Result
$1M+ managed across 25 concurrent campaigns. Successful DSP migration. Hiring contribution still employed.
Allocated and optimized $1M+ in paid media spend for Lilly, Pfizer, and J&J in a regulated environment. Managed 25 concurrent campaigns without performance lapses during the DSP migration. Recommended a jr. email marketing hire during the tenure — that person is still employed at POCN. Left voluntarily when THIS had grown enough to support full-time.
